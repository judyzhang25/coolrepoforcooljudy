About this page

An increase in a city's population surgest the number of emergency call volume and response rate. Trends in existing data may assist our services in being better prepared and more readily available to handle emergency situations. The SF Data Analytics team has prepared a series of graphs and metrics to provide statistics and additional information regarding the quality of current services. This page features topics such as:

  • Facts and Queries

  • Average Ambulance Response Time vs Time of Day

Facts

Some areas of San Francisco experience a higher level of incidents than others. From past data, we can analyze certain trends, such as which services are most required in which areas or what time of day are calls most frequently placed, to predict needs and determine where our services are best allocated.


One metric we have taken note of is that the zipcode area of 94127 takes the longest to dispatch to, with an average time of 44.823 minutes. The most common unit to dispatch to this zipcode is fire engines. A strategy to reduce travel time is having our 911 dispatchers notify units of estimated time arrival and potential delays when dispatching them to this neighborhood. The team may be advised to pay greater attention to traffic conditions or take advantage of local shortcuts in order to avoid unforeseen accidents. Specifically when fire engines are dispatched, the department may advised to gain more familiarity with the area and heighten their sense of urgency when heading to this area.


Predicting where our units need to be at what time is also crucial to handling emergency situations. We, here at the Data Analytics team, have generated a simulator that evaluates past incidents and predicts which unit is most needed given the address and the time of day.
